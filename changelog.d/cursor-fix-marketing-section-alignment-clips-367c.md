## Marketing "The Shift" + "Meet Vela" alignment/clipping (branch `cursor/fix-marketing-section-alignment-clips-367c`) — 2026-07-12

**Trigger**: Two cosmetic layout bugs on the landing page — "The Shift" rendered small/left-crammed and the following "Add them…" panel cut into its last line, and the "Meet Vela" chat card had its top border and "An example of how Vela responds" header clipped by the sticky nav.

`[FIXED]` **Restored vertical rhythm on the two bare `.container` marketing sections and centred "The Shift".** Root cause for both: `<section className="… container …">` — `.container`'s `padding: 0 32px` (specificity 0,1,0) out-specifies the global `section { padding-block: clamp(76px,12vh,140px) }` (0,0,1) and zeroes the sections' top/bottom padding (the same trap already documented for `.close` in `globals.css`). With no padding, `#shift` had no breathing room and the following `.how` panel (also a zero-padding `.container`) butted its bordered top edge into "…built for *them*.", while `#vela`'s `.vela-card` sat flush at the section top where the `position: sticky` nav (~68px) overlaid and clipped it. Fix: give `.shift` and a new `.vela-section` their own `padding-block` (equal specificity, later in source, so it wins the top/bottom back while `.container` keeps the horizontal gutters) plus `scroll-margin-top` so in-page anchor jumps land below the nav; centre `.shift` (`text-align: center` + auto-margined body). Styling/layout only — no copy or structural changes. Verified centred and fully visible with no clipping at desktop and 320/375/390.
