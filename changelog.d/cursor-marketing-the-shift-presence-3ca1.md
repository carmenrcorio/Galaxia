## Marketing "The Shift" — give the thesis section real presence (branch `cursor/marketing-the-shift-presence-3ca1`) — 2026-07-12

**Trigger**: "The Shift" ("Astrology forgot the people you love." + its paragraph) — the page's THESIS — rendered as a small, narrow, centred island floating in a large empty band, with the least visual presence of any section despite being the most important. A prior fix (`cursor/fix-marketing-section-alignment-clips-367c`) corrected its clipping but over-corrected into a shrunken centred column. Styling/layout only — no copy changes. Untouched per §2: `next.config.mjs`, `.npmrc`, Vercel settings.

**Phase 0 — why it rendered small (confirmed before changing anything).** `WhySection` is `<section className="shift container">`, and `.shift` in `globals.css` set `max-width: 880px` + `text-align: center`, with `.shift .body { max-width: 62ch; margin: 22px auto 0 }`. So its content column was ~300px narrower than the page's standard `.container` (`max-width: 1180px`), auto-centred, and the body auto-centred within that — a narrow floating stripe. The adjacent sections are full-width and left-aligned: the hero (`.hero.container`, 1180px, left/`start`) and the "Add them…" panel (`.how-panel` inside a 1180px `.container`, left-aligned). The dominant cause of the "tiny" headline: `.shift h2` had **no `font-size`** and fell back to the UA default (~24px/1.5em). The reference had a global `h2{font-size:clamp(2.1rem,5vw,3.5rem)}` (`design/reference/galaxia-landing-v2.html`), but the JSX rebuild only ported the reference's `h1,h2,h3` family/weight/letter-spacing into `.marketing h1,h2,h3` and dropped the size — so the thesis headline was ~24px while every sibling headline sets its own clamp (`.edge-h2` clamp(2.3rem,5vw,3.9rem), `.vela-h2`, `.close-h`). Measured live: pre-fix `.shift` width 880px centred, `.shift h2` 24px.

`[FIXED]` **Gave "The Shift" the visual weight and alignment of the rest of the page.**
- **Width**: dropped `max-width: 880px` so `.shift` uses the shared `.container` 1180px content column — identical to the hero (both measure width 1180, left 130 at 1440px).
- **Alignment**: removed `text-align: center` and the body's `auto` centring (`margin: 22px 0 0`); the section is now left-aligned within the content column, matching the hero and `.how-head` (chosen over centring because that is the page's established rhythm — the eyebrow, headline and body now share the same left edge, x=162 at 1440px, as the hero H1 and `.edge-h2`).
- **Size**: added an explicit `.shift h2` headline size `clamp(2.3rem, 5.5vw, 4.1rem)` (+ `line-height: 1.04`, `letter-spacing: -.02em`, `max-width: 20ch`) so the thesis reads as the page's lead statement — measured 65.6px at desktop (≥ hero H1 57.6px, `.edge-h2` 62.4px) and 36.8px at 375px (= `.edge-h2`).
- **Vertical space**: kept `padding-block: clamp(76px,12vh,140px)` (the standard `section` rhythm, restated because `.container`'s `padding:0 32px` zeroes it) — with real content presence the band no longer reads as empty. `scroll-margin-top` retained for anchor jumps.

**Verified**: `@galaxia/web` `tsc --noEmit` + `next build` pass. Live browser (Playwright, `.reveal` forced visible): desktop `.shift` now matches `.hero` width/left exactly and its headline exceeds sibling headlines; no horizontal overflow at 320/375/390, full-width and left-aligned at each, no shrunken island.
